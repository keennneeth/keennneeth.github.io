Human Computer Interaction

Project 1: Elevator Project
Kenneth Rodriguez

Issues and Design

There are a couple of bad options used in this elevator. One being the rear doors, to keep it simple it should just open one way, or have both doors open at the same time. Having double of everything makes it very cluttered, double numbers and double door open/close. Overall it is not the worst design invented, after looking at it for a couple seconds you can understand it.

Every Day Use

People will typically use elevators in order to avoid stairs in very tall buildings, some rare times i'd use an elevator is maybe moving large objects and I don't want to walk up the stairs. This interface makes it very practical since you just click a button and it will bring you up.

Feedback

Many elevators will have the same functions, click a floor and it will do the rest. This elevator will provide feedback by highlighting the floor in red, that's when the user will know that it has been activated.

Common Mistakes

One very common mistake that people will make with this design is opening the rear doors instead of the front door or clicking the open door button and opening the wrong side.

Solution

Simple yet effective, the new design will include a screen that will display floor level at the top of the door. The control panel will consist of 5 buttons and a screen that will display destination level. User will use up and down button to choose floor level and hit green button to go to desired floor.

Design

The reason for this is because it will allow for a clean interface and east to comprehend, using the up and down arrows will allow the user to pick a desired floor and after a couple seconds of being on that number the door will automatically close or they can click the green button to speed up the process. This will eliminate the close door button, and a certain amount of buttons depending on how tall the building is.

Design Options

This can be made with a touch screen if desired, or just simple buttons, both will operate the same but one will be more modern. The screen will be about 6" tall and 10" width, The reason for this is because the screen doesnt have to be large in order to fit 2 buttons and a number. The feedback for this will be a speaker announcing "Going Up", or "Going down", depending on the situation.
An information screen can be brought up by clicking a series of buttons that will display the maintence info and anything important that can be needed.
